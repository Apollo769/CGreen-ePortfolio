Professional Self-Assessment

Throughout the Computer Science program, I developed a foundation in software development, software engineering, databases, networking, and cybersecurity. While I originally entered the program with an interest in information technology, networking, and cybersecurity, my coursework helped me discover a strong interest in software development and software security. Through projects, coursework, and the capstone experience, I developed both the technical and professional skills necessary to pursue a career in technology. This ePortfolio reflects not only the completion of my degree but also the growth of my knowledge, skills, and professional interests over the past three years.

One of the most important lessons I learned throughout the program was the value of communication and collaboration. Effective technology solutions require more than technical knowledge; they require the ability to communicate ideas, requirements, and design decisions to different audiences. Through software design documents, technical reports, project narratives, and presentations, I strengthened my ability to explain technical concepts clearly and professionally. Collaboration was also reinforced through peer discussions, instructor feedback, project reviews, and workplace experiences. These opportunities taught me how to evaluate feedback, communicate technical decisions, and work toward solutions that meet requirements.

The program strengthened my understanding of software development principles, programming languages, and software engineering practices. Through coursework, I gained experience developing applications in Java, Python, C++, JavaScript, HTML, and CSS while learning how different technologies can be applied to solve real-world problems. Projects involving Android application development and full-stack web development using the MEAN stack provided experience designing, implementing, testing, and maintaining software systems. These experiences reinforced the importance of maintainability, scalability, separation of concerns, and thoughtful architectural design.

In addition to software development, I gained experience working with data structures, algorithms, and databases. Through coursework and project development, I applied concepts such as searching, sorting, filtering, performance optimization, and data management while working with SQLite, MongoDB, and Firebase Firestore. These experiences strengthened my ability to evaluate technical trade-offs and select solutions that best align with project requirements. They also reinforced the relationship between efficient algorithms, effective data organization, and overall software performance.

Security and networking were among the most influential areas of study throughout the program. Through networking and cybersecurity coursework, I gained knowledge related to network design, firewalls, virtual local area networks (VLANs), access controls, authentication systems, compliance considerations, and secure software development practices. These experiences reinforced the importance of integrating security throughout the software development lifecycle and contributed to my growing interest in software security as a future career path.

The program also introduced me to artificial intelligence, machine learning, and emerging technologies. Through coursework involving neural networks, reinforcement learning, and intelligent systems, I gained an appreciation for how artificial intelligence can be used to solve complex problems and support processes. Studying these technologies broadened my understanding of modern computing while helping me recognize both the opportunities and challenges associated with technological advancements.

The artifacts included in this ePortfolio demonstrate the culmination of the knowledge and skills developed throughout the program. First, the software engineering and design enhancement highlights my ability to improve software architecture, maintainability, validation, and authentication practices. Second, the algorithms and data structures enhancement demonstrates my understanding of data organization, performance optimization, searching, sorting, and filtering techniques. Lastly, the database enhancement shows my ability to implement cloud-based technologies, secure authentication systems, and user-specific data management solutions. Together, these artifacts demonstrate how software engineering, algorithms, databases, cloud technologies, and security principles work together to create effective, maintainable, and secure software systems.

Overall, the Computer Science program prepared me to enter the technology field with a diverse technical skill set and a deeper understanding of how software, databases, networking, security, and emerging technologies interact within modern computing environments. More importantly, it taught me how to approach complex problems, evaluate technical trade-offs, communicate effectively, and continuously learn as technology evolves. The experiences and knowledge gained throughout the program have prepared me for continued professional growth and will serve as the foundation for a career in software development and software security.
